# Product — Nexo Vending

## Register
**Brand.** This is a marketing landing for a B2B physical product. Design IS the product. Every visual decision is a sales argument.

## What Nexo Vending is
Premium vending machines installed in companies under a comodato model (Nexo invests, the company hosts, end-users pay per consumption). Three product lines:

1. **Nexo Café** — specialty coffee, 8 hot beverages, premium 7oz cups, for offices and coworking spaces.
2. **Nexo Protein** — instant protein shakes for high-traffic gyms.
3. **Nexo Snacks** — curated premium snacks for offices.

We don't sell coffee. We sell a tech-enabled wellbeing experience at the point of consumption.

## Who buys
Decision-makers in Medellín, Colombia:
- Office managers and HR directors at 50-500 person companies
- Coworking operators
- Gym owners (mid to large)
- Facilities heads at corporate buildings

They evaluate on: signal of company culture, zero-friction operation, modern aesthetic, time-to-install.

## Tone
- Direct and confident. No hedging.
- Benefit before feature, always.
- Tuteo corporativo (informal "tú", professional context).
- Short sentences. Periods earn their place.
- Spanish (Colombia). Never English borrowing unless the term is technical (e.g. "vending", "espresso").
- No em dashes. Commas, colons, periods, parentheses.

## Anti-references
What this is NOT:
- Generic vending company website (CSV-row tables, stock photos, "we are a leading provider")
- Coffee chain marketing (cozy mugs, plant-on-table photography)
- Generic SaaS landing (3-card grids, hero-metric template, gradient text)
- Crypto/Web3 aesthetic (purple glows, neon, holographic)
- Fitness brand aggression (red, all-caps, "TRANSFORM YOUR LIFE")

## Must-NOT-do
- Fake social proof (no fake company logos, no invented testimonials, no "50+ clients" until that's real)
- Em dashes
- Emojis in product UI (icons or SVGs only)
- Gradient text on headlines
- Cards as default container (only when truly best affordance)
- Centered everything (variance > 4 forbids centered hero/sections)
- 3-column equal-card grids (banned signature pattern)

## Strategic principles
1. **Show the machine, but show the result more.** Hero must convey "energized team" not "vending box".
2. **Conversion paths everywhere.** Hero CTA, per-product CTA, sticky/floating CTA, post-promise CTA, final form. All with first-person verbs ("Quiero mi máquina").
3. **Trust before excitement.** Trust signals (48h, $0, mantenimiento incluido) appear near every CTA, not just at the bottom.
4. **Diferenciador no es el producto, es la operación.** Cero logística para el cliente es el verdadero argumento.
